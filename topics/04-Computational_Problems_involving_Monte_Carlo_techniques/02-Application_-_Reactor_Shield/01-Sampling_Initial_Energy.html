Sampling Initial Energy

This step requires a few lines of calculas ...

The initial energy of the particles is in interval \([E_{\min},E_{\max}]\) and follows distribution, \(1/\sqrt{E}\). To model this we need to:

  • Define PDF, the probability density function, \(f(E)\).
    This will depend on a scaling constant, \(c\) so that the integral under curve is one.
  • Derive CDF, the cumulative probability function, \(F(E)\).
    This is just (hopefully) simple calculus to derive \(F\).
  • Derive inverse CDF so we can apply the Inverse transform sampling method

Task 1A — Derive the Cumulative Probability Function and its inverse

Hence, starting with probability density function \[ f(E) = \begin{cases} 0 & E < E_{\min}\\ c/\sqrt{E} & E_{\min} \le E < E_{\max}\\ 0 & E_{\max} \le E\ \end{cases} \]

Show that the inverse CDF is \[ F^{-1}(u) = \left(\frac{u}{2c} +\sqrt{E_{\min}}\right)^2\qquad\text{where}\qquad c = \frac{1}{2\left(\sqrt{E_{\max}}-\sqrt{E_{\max}}\right)} \]

Task 1B — Derive the expected value and the variance of the CDF

Derive the mean and variance.

Task 1C — Verify generated samples follows the required distribution

Now to sample values, we sample \(u\sim{\cal U}(0,1)\), then \(E=F^{-1}(u)\) will follow the required distribution.
